Follow up on my variegated Jade water propagations 🍭(the leaves remind me of candy). These actually rooted within a couple of weeks but I’m slow at updates lately so this is after a couple months (swipe to see). The two single leaves have started sprouting new growth. The top cutting has roots and the little leaves on top have started to get bigger 🥳 They’re all more than ready to pot in soil. Here are some things I do when I pot up propagations - especially ones with really delicate roots: 🪴Pick a small pot - this is to avoid root rot because a smaller pot means less potting mix to retain water, so the little roots have a better chance of not drowning 🪴Add perlite to my regular potting mix - perlite retains moisture and aerates the soil so the roots can get the air and water they need 🪴Pre-moisten the potting mix - I keep my potting mix in a storage tote and I’ll pour some water in and mix it up so the soil isn’t bone dry when I use it. Really dry soil doesn’t soak up water very well, which makes it difficult to water later on 🪴Don’t compact the soil - roots are delicate so you want to be careful not to break them by packing in the soil. Roots also like some air so it helps to keep the soil light 🪴Thoroughly water - the roots are used to being surrounded by moisture so watering right away can help them adapt to their new home. Watering also lets the soil settle around the roots. 🪴Place in a clear bag or container - this keeps it warm and humid while the plant adjusts to soil.
